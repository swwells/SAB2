SAB Highlights: Ezra - Isaiah

Ezra | Nehemiah | Esther | Job | Psalms | Proverbs | Ecclesiastes | Song of Solomon | Isaiah

    Ezra

  1. Check out the contradictions between the boring lists given in Ezra 2 and Neh.7.

  2. The Israelites offend God by "taking" foreign wives and thereby corrupting the "holy seed." 9:2

  3. When Ezra hears of the intermarriages he tears his clothes, plucks out his hair and beard, and sits down astonished. 9:3

  4. Ezra tells the men that they must abandon their "strange" wives and children if they want to avoid God's wrath. 10:2-3, 10-12

    Nehemiah

  5. Check out the contradictions between the boring lists given in Ezra 2 and Neh.7.

  6. Nehemiah rebukes the men for marrying "strange wives." To punish them he "contended with them, and cursed them, and smote certain of them, and plucked off their hair." 13:25-27

    Esther

  7. King Ahasuerus throws a party and encourages his guests to drink to excess. Then, when they are all drunk, he orders Queen Vashti to show her stuff before him and his guests. 1:7-11

  8. Queen Vashti (the real heroine of the Book of Esther) refuses to display herself in front of the king's drunken guests. 1:7-12

  9. For her refusal, Vashti is stripped of her crown and came "no more before king." 1:13-19

  10. Because of Vashti's disobedience the king decrees that "all the wives shall give to their husbands honor" and "that every man should bear rule over his own house." 1:20-22

  11. "All the fair young virgins" throughout the kingdom are brought before the king and the one that "pleaseth" him the most will replace Queen Vashti. 2:2-4

  12. Esther wins the pleasing the king babe contest. 2:8-9

  13. But since women are dirty, she must be "purified" for twelve months before she can be made queen. 2:12

  14. Esther has Haman and his ten sons hung. 7:10, 9:13

  15. At Esther's request, the king orders everyone in the empire who planned to kill Jews to be killed by Jews, along with their wives and children. 8:9-19

  16. The Jews kill everyone who ever had a bad thought toward them, along with their Jew-hating families. 9:1-16

    Job

  17. Job was a perfect man, the richest in all the east. He had 7 sons, 3 daughters, thousands of animals, and many slaves. 1:1-4

  18. God makes a bet with his son, Satan, telling Satan to do nasty things to Job to see if he can get Job to curse God to his face. 1:6-12

  19. Job's slaves and animals are burned to death or killed with swords. Then Job's children are killed in a windstorm while partying. 1:13-19

  20. God kills (or allows Satan to kill) Job's children, but Job doesn't "foolishly" blame God. 1:20-22

  21. He allows Satan to torment Job, just to see how he will react. 2:3-7

  22. He gives Job to Satan, saying: "Behold, all that he hath is in thy power." 2:6

  23. Satan (or God) smites Job with boils from the "sole of his foot unto his crown." 2:7

  24. Job's wife says that to retain his integrity he should "curse God and die." 2:9

  25. Job curses the day he was born. 3:1-2, 3:11

  26. God multiplies wounds without cause, destroys the perfect along with the wicked, laughs at the trial of the innocent, and hides the truth from judges. 9:17-24

  27. After God and Satan get done tormenting Job, God gave Job even more stuff than he had before, including children. God gave Job another set of 10 kids, with even prettier daughters! 42:13-15
